# The Common Room

The Common Room is where the magic happens - it's your game's public discussion forum where characters interact, plan, debate, and develop the story together. Think of it as the "town square" of your game.

## What is the Common Room?

The Common Room is a **public discussion space** where all active characters can:
- Post in-character conversations
- Respond to each other's posts
- Plan strategies and coordinate actions
- Develop relationships and drama
- React to game events

**Key Points:**
- Everyone in the game can see Common Room posts (including the GM)
- Posts are written as your character (not as yourself)
- It's the primary way to interact with other players between action phases
- Available during both Common Room phases AND Action phases

## Common Room Phases

Games alternate between Common Room phases and Action phases. During a **Common Room Phase**:

- ✅ This is the "main" discussion time
- ✅ Players are expected to be active
- ✅ Great for planning before the next Action phase
- ✅ No action submissions yet - just talk and coordinate

**But you can also post during Action phases!** The Common Room never closes - it's just less active since players are busy writing their actions.

## Making Your First Post

### Writing a Post

1. Navigate to your game page
2. Click the **Common Room** tab
3. Click **New Post** or **Create Post**
4. Write your post as your character
5. Click **Post** to publish

**Example First Post:**
> *Mara pushes through the tavern door, shaking rain from her cloak. She scans the room, eyes adjusting to the dim light, and makes her way to an empty table near the fire.*
>
> "Well, this is a sorry looking bunch," she mutters, loud enough to be heard. "Anyone here actually know what we're getting into, or are we all wandering blind?"

### Post Tips

**Do:**
- ✅ Write in character (use first or third person)
- ✅ Describe actions and reactions
- ✅ Ask questions and engage others
- ✅ React to what others have said
- ✅ Move the conversation forward

**Don't:**
- ❌ Post out-of-character chat (save that for private messages)
- ❌ Write a novel - keep it digestible
- ❌ Ignore what others have posted
- ❌ Make decisions for other characters
- ❌ Reveal information your character wouldn't know

## Responding to Posts

### Using Comments

Every post can have threaded comments:

1. Click **Reply** or **Comment** on any post
2. Write your character's response
3. Click **Submit**

Comments create threaded conversations, making it easy to follow specific discussions within the larger room.

**Example Exchange:**
> **Original Post (Marcus):**
> "I've heard rumors about the old mansion on the hill. Missing people, strange lights. We should investigate."
>
> **Comment (Elena):**
> "Investigate? You mean break in? I'm a scientist, not a criminal!"
>
> **Comment (Marcus):**
> "It's not breaking in if we knock first. Besides, don't you want to know what's really happening?"
>
> **Comment (Chen):**
> "I'm with Marcus. We need answers, and the authorities aren't going to provide them."

### Mentioning Other Characters

You can reference other characters using `@charactername`:

> "I agree with @Marcus - we need to act. But @Elena has a point about legality. What if we approach it during the day, claim we're concerned neighbors?"

Some games support automatic notifications when you mention a character.

## Common Room Etiquette

### Be a Good Player

**Engage Actively:**
- Respond to others' posts within a reasonable time
- Don't leave people hanging in conversations
- Create hooks for others to interact with
- Read what others have posted before jumping in

**Respect the Tone:**
- Match the game's style (serious, comedic, dramatic, etc.)
- Don't derail serious moments with jokes (unless that's your character)
- Respect when the GM is setting up something important

**Share the Spotlight:**
- Don't dominate every conversation
- Give quieter players space to contribute
- Ask questions that let others shine
- Respond to posts from less active players

### Character Voice

Your posts should sound like your character, not you:

**Weak (sounds like a player):**
> "Hey everyone, I think we should check out that mansion. It seems like the main quest."

**Strong (sounds like a character):**
> *Kira leans forward, fingers drumming on the table.* "That mansion? I've been past it twice. Gave me the creeps both times. But if there's something there worth finding..." *She grins.* "I'm in."

**Elements of Good Character Voice:**
- Action descriptions (what they're doing physically)
- Internal thoughts or reactions
- Speech patterns consistent with their background
- Emotional responses fitting their personality

## Types of Posts

### Planning & Strategy

Coordinating with other characters before actions:

> "Alright, here's what I'm thinking. @Theo, you're good with tech - can you handle the security system? @Rina and I will create a distraction at the front. @Kai, you'll need to get to the server room while everyone's looking our way."

### Character Development

Revealing personality, backstory, or relationships:

> *Serena stares into her drink, unusually quiet.* "You know, I used to live near here. Before... everything." *She shakes her head.* "Doesn't matter. That life's gone."

### Reactions to Events

Responding to GM updates or phase results:

> "Did you all see what the GM posted? The mansion is *owned* by the mayor? This changes everything. We can't just break in - he could have us arrested."

### Social Interaction

Just characters being themselves:

> *Jax throws his feet up on the table and leans back.* "So, Marcus. What's your story? You don't strike me as the 'concerned citizen' type. What's really got you interested in this case?"

## During Different Phases

### Common Room Phase

This is prime time for the Common Room:

- **Main Activity**: Planning, discussing, coordinating
- **Goal**: Prepare for the next Action phase
- **Pacing**: Can be fast and active
- **GM Involvement**: May post updates, NPCs, or prompts

**What to do:**
1. Review what happened in the last Results phase
2. Discuss new information
3. Debate strategies and options
4. Make plans for the upcoming Action phase
5. Develop character relationships

### Action Phase

The Common Room is still available, just quieter:

- **Main Activity**: Players are writing their action submissions
- **Purpose**: Last-minute coordination or quick questions
- **Pacing**: Slower than Common Room phase
- **Posts**: Usually shorter and more focused

**What to do:**
1. Clarify any final questions
2. Coordinate timing if actions involve multiple characters
3. Handle any quick character moments
4. Generally, focus on writing your action!

### Results Phase

Brief period while GM writes results:

- **Usually very short** (hours to a day)
- Common Room typically quiet
- Players waiting for results before discussing
- Good time to catch up on reading old posts

## Advanced Features

### Editing Posts

Most games allow editing your posts:
- Fix typos
- Clarify confusing phrasing
- Add missed details

**But don't:**
- Change what your character said after others responded
- Retcon major plot points
- Abuse editing to "take back" character decisions

### Deleting Posts

Usually reserved for:
- Duplicate posts
- Accidental out-of-character content
- Posts made in error

Check with your GM before deleting posts others have responded to.

### Formatting

Use markdown for better posts:

```markdown
*Italics for actions and thoughts*
**Bold for emphasis**

> "Quoted speech"

---
Horizontal rule for scene breaks
```

### Private Side Conversations

If you need to talk to just one or two characters:
- Use **Private Messages** instead of the Common Room
- See: [Private Messaging Guide](./private-messages.md)
- The GM cannot see private messages (by design)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### The Silent Observer
❌ Reading posts but never contributing
✅ Even quiet characters can nod, grunt, or react

### The Novelist
❌ 2000-word posts that no one reads
✅ Break long content into multiple posts or comments

### The Metagamer
❌ "As I mentioned in the private message @Marcus sent me..."
✅ Only reference information your character knows publicly

### The Steamroller
❌ Making plans and assuming everyone agrees
✅ Ask for input and give others space to object

### The Ghost
❌ Posting once, then disappearing for days
✅ Check in regularly, even if just to say your character is listening

## Tips for Success

### Make Your Posts Engaging

**Include:**
- Physical actions and descriptions
- Emotional reactions
- Questions or prompts for others
- Connections to earlier posts

**Example:**
> *Sarah's eyes widen as she reads the note again.* "Wait, wait. This symbol - I've seen it before." *She rummages through her bag, pulling out a worn journal.* "Here! Same mark, from that case three years ago. The one that was never solved."
>
> *She looks around the table.* "Does anyone else think that's too much of a coincidence?"

### Timing Your Posts

**Good times to post:**
- After the GM posts an update (react!)
- When someone asks your character a direct question
- When you have new information or a plan to share
- During active discussions (strike while hot)

**Consider waiting:**
- If the conversation is moving fast and you'd interrupt
- If quieter players haven't had a chance to respond yet
- If the GM seems to be building to something

### Reading the Room

Pay attention to:
- The GM's posts (they're steering the story)
- Who's been active vs. quiet (engage the quiet ones)
- The tone of the conversation (match it)
- Unresolved questions (help answer them)

## Working with the GM

### GM Posts in Common Room

GMs can post as:
- **NPCs** (non-player characters you can interact with)
- **Narration** (describing scenes or events)
- **Updates** (new information or prompts)

When the GM posts, pay attention! It's usually important.

### Asking the GM Questions

**Good questions:**
- "Is there a library in town we could research at?"
- "What time of day is it now?"
- "Can my character see the mansion from here?"

**Better as private messages or in your action:**
- Secret plans you don't want others to know
- Questions about your specific character's abilities
- Requests for private information

## Sample Common Room Session

Here's what a typical Common Room conversation might look like:

---

**GM**: *The rain has stopped, leaving the streets muddy and dark. It's nearly midnight. Those of you in the tavern can hear the church bell striking twelve.*

**Marcus**: *Stands and stretches.* "Well, if we're doing this, now's the time. The streets are empty, everyone's asleep. Perfect conditions."

**Elena**: "I still think this is a terrible idea." *But she's already gathering her equipment.* "At least let me bring my medical kit. When - not if, WHEN - this goes wrong, I'd rather not bleed out."

**Chen**: *Nods quietly.* "I'll take point. I know these streets. If we're spotted, follow my lead - I know places to hide."

**Kira**: *Grins and checks her lockpicks.* "This is the best idea we've had all week. @Marcus, you sure about that window on the east side?"

**Marcus**: "As sure as I can be without actually breaking in already. Saw it yesterday - no bars, dodgy latch. Should be our way in."

**Elena**: "Should be. Great. Those are the words I love to hear right before a criminal act." *Sighs.* "Fine. But we stick together. No one goes off alone. Agreed?"

**Chen**: "Agreed. We go in as a group, we leave as a group."
