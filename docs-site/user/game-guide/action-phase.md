# The Action Phase

Action Phases are where the real gameplay happens - this is when you tell the GM what your character does, and they respond with results. It's the heart of the turn-based gameplay loop.

## What is an Action Phase?

An Action Phase is a **private submission period** where:
- Each player writes what their character does
- Submissions are private (only you and the GM see them)
- You have until the deadline to submit or revise
- The GM reads all actions, then writes individualized results
- After results, the game moves to the next Common Room phase

**The Cycle:**
```
Common Room → ACTION PHASE → Results → (new Common Room) → ACTION PHASE → ...
```

## How Action Phases Work

### Timeline

1. **Phase Starts**: GM creates an Action Phase with a deadline
2. **Submission Period**: Players write and submit their actions
3. **Deadline**: Submissions lock (no more changes)
4. **GM Processing**: GM reads all actions and writes results
5. **Results Delivered**: Each player receives private results
6. **Phase Ends**: Game moves to Common Room or next Action Phase

**Typical Duration:** 3-7 days, depending on your GM's pacing

## Writing Your Action

### Accessing the Action Phase

1. Go to your game page
2. Click the **Actions** tab or **Current Phase** section
3. Look for **Submit Action** or **Your Action**
4. Write your action in the text box
5. Click **Submit** or **Save**

### What to Include

Your action should describe:

**What your character does:**
> "I search the library for information about the symbol we found."

**How they do it:**
> "I start with the occult section, looking for books on ancient symbols and secret societies. I'll be methodical - checking indexes, cross-referencing anything promising."

**What they're trying to accomplish:**
> "I'm hoping to find out what this symbol means and whether it's connected to the disappearances."

**Any special resources or skills:**
> "I'm using my Research skill and my knowledge of mythology. I also have that old journal from my mentor that might have relevant notes."

### Example Action Submission

**Good Example:**
> **Action: Investigate the Library**
>
> I wait until the library closes at 9 PM, then slip in through the basement window I noticed yesterday. Once inside, I head straight for the Special Collections room - that's where they keep the old town records.
>
> I'm looking for anything about the mansion on Hillcrest Drive: property records, previous owners, any newspaper articles about strange events. I'll photograph important pages with my phone rather than risk stealing documents.
>
> If I hear anyone coming, I'll hide behind the filing cabinets near the back. I've got my lockpick set if any cabinets are locked.
>
> I'll spend about two hours searching, then leave the same way I came in before the night security does their midnight rounds.
>
> **Goal:** Find historical information about the mansion and its previous occupants.

### Action Writing Tips

**Be Specific:**
- ❌ "I investigate the mansion"
- ✅ "I watch the mansion from across the street between 8-10 PM, noting who comes and goes, what lights turn on, and any unusual activity"

**Include Contingencies:**
- ❌ "I break in through the window"
- ✅ "I try to open the window. If it's locked, I'll use my lockpicks. If I can't pick it, I'll try the side door instead"

**State Your Intent:**
- ❌ "I talk to the mayor"
- ✅ "I try to get the mayor alone and ask him about the symbol, watching his reaction carefully to see if he recognizes it"

**Use Your Character's Abilities:**
- ❌ "I search the room"
- ✅ "I use my detective training to search methodically - checking for hidden compartments, disturbed dust, anything out of place"

## Privacy and Visibility

### Who Sees What

**Your Action Submission:**
- ✅ You can see it
- ✅ The GM can see it (after deadline)
- ❌ Other players CANNOT see it
- ❌ GM cannot see it BEFORE the deadline

**Important:** Your action is completely private. Other players don't know what you're doing unless you told them in the Common Room or via Private Message.

### GM Visibility

The GM **cannot see actions during the submission period**. This is by design:
- Prevents the GM from being influenced by early submissions
- Ensures all players have equal time to revise
- Keeps everyone on a level playing field
- GM sees all actions at once after the deadline

**After the deadline**, the GM sees all actions and can start writing results.

## Editing Your Action

### Before the Deadline

You can edit your action as many times as you want:

1. Go to your action submission
2. Click **Edit** or **Revise**
3. Make your changes
4. Click **Save** or **Update**

**Common reasons to edit:**
- You thought of a better approach
- Another player's Common Room post changed your plans
- You realized you forgot to mention something important
- You want to add more detail

### After the Deadline

**Actions are locked.** You cannot edit after the deadline passes.

If you absolutely need to change something:
- Send a private message to the GM immediately
- Explain the situation
- The GM may allow a minor clarification
- Don't expect major changes to be accepted

## Deadlines

### Understanding Deadlines

Every Action Phase has a deadline displayed:
- In your timezone (automatically converted)
- Shows countdown timer
- Usually several days away
- Set by the GM

**Example:**
```
Deadline: November 18, 2024 at 11:59 PM PST
Time Remaining: 3 days, 5 hours
```

### What Happens at Deadline

When the deadline hits:
- ✅ All actions lock (no more edits)
- ✅ GM can now see all submissions
- ✅ Late submissions are not accepted
- ✅ If you didn't submit, your character does nothing

### Missing the Deadline

If you don't submit an action:
- Your character takes no action this phase
- The story continues without you
- You miss that phase's events and information
- You can still participate in the next phase

**Avoid this by:**
- Setting personal reminders
- Submitting early (you can always edit)
- Communicating with GM if you need an extension

### Extension Requests

If you need more time:
1. Contact your GM as soon as possible
2. Explain why you need an extension
3. Be respectful - GMs are volunteers
4. Don't expect automatic approval

The GM might:
- Grant a short extension for everyone
- Allow you to submit late in special circumstances
- Decline if too many players are waiting

## Coordinating with Other Players

### Planning in Common Room

Before the Action Phase:
- Discuss plans in the Common Room
- Agree on who does what
- Set meeting points or timing
- Share important information

**Example Common Room Discussion:**
> **Marcus:** "Alright, here's the plan. @Kira, you'll pick the lock on the back door at exactly 11 PM. @Elena, you're on lookout - text if anyone approaches. @Chen and I will search the office."
>
> **Kira:** "Got it. 11 PM, back door. I'll need about 90 seconds for that lock."
>
> **Elena:** "I'll be across the street with binoculars. You'll have plenty of warning."

### In Your Action

Reference the plan:
> "As agreed with the team, I position myself across the street from the mansion at 10:45 PM. At 11:00, I watch Kira approach the back door. I keep my phone ready to text if I see anyone approaching..."

### When Plans Change

If your action differs from the plan:
- The GM will handle it
- Maybe you arrive early, or take a different approach
- Other players won't know until results
- This can create interesting drama!

## Types of Actions

### Investigation
Searching, researching, gathering information

**Example:**
> "I spend the day at the town hall, going through property records for the Hillcrest mansion. I'll look for: previous owners, sales history, any liens or legal issues, and building permits. I'll take photos of anything relevant."

### Social Interaction
Talking to NPCs, interrogating, persuading

**Example:**
> "I approach the mayor at the town fundraiser, casual and friendly. After some small talk, I mention that I've been researching local history and ask if he knows anything about the Hillcrest mansion. I'll watch his body language carefully - does he seem nervous? Evasive?"

### Stealth/Infiltration
Sneaking, breaking in, avoiding detection

**Example:**
> "I wait until 2 AM, then approach the mansion from the back, staying in the shadows. I'll check the back door first - if it's locked, I'll try the basement window. I move slowly and pause often to listen. If I hear anything, I'll freeze and wait."

### Combat/Confrontation
Direct action, forcing an issue

**Example:**
> "I confront the mayor directly. I show him the symbol and tell him I know he's involved. I'm not threatening, but I'm firm - I want answers, and I'm not leaving without them. My hand is near my pepper spray, just in case."

### Preparation
Setting up for future phases, gathering resources

**Example:**
> "I spend the day preparing: I buy a good flashlight, a lock pick set, and a portable scanner. I print out maps of the mansion property. I text @Chen to make sure we're still on for tomorrow night. I also research the security company that monitors the mansion."

## Receiving Your Results

### What to Expect

After the Action Phase deadline passes and you submit your action, the GM will:
- Read all player actions
- Determine what happens
- Write individual results for each player
- Send results privately

**Results usually include:**
- What happened when you tried your action
- What you discovered or learned
- Any consequences or complications
- Information relevant to the story
- Setup for future phases

### Reading Your Results

When results arrive:
1. You'll get a notification
2. Go to your game page
3. Click **Results** or **GM Messages**
4. Read your private result

**Your results are private** - other players don't see them unless you share.

### Example Result

**Your Action Was:**
> "I search the library's special collections for information about the symbol."

**GM's Result:**
> You find three references to the symbol in the special collections:
>
> 1. An 1887 newspaper article about a "secret society" that used the symbol, disbanded after a scandal
> 2. A town history book mentions the symbol appearing on the Hillcrest mansion gates when it was built
> 3. A journal from a previous librarian (1923) notes seeing the symbol "carved into the oak tree near Miller's Creek"
>
> The librarian's journal also mentions that anyone who investigated the symbol seemed to leave town suddenly. There are five names listed, all crossed out with red ink.
>
> As you're reading, you notice a man watching you from across the reading room. When you make eye contact, he quickly leaves. You didn't get a good look at him.

### Sharing Results

You can choose to:
- Keep results completely private
- Share with specific players (via Private Message)
- Share with everyone (in Common Room)
- Share selectively (some info public, some private)

The GM cannot force you to share results with other players.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### The Vague Action
❌ "I investigate the mansion"
✅ "I examine the mansion's exterior, looking for security cameras, checking all ground-floor windows and doors for signs of entry"

### The Novel
❌ 3000 words of backstory and internal monologue
✅ Focus on what you DO, with enough detail for the GM to adjudicate

### The Multi-Action
❌ "I search the library, then the town hall, then interview the mayor, then break into the mansion..."
✅ Pick ONE main thing to focus on. You can't do everything in one phase.

### The Assumption
❌ "After I successfully pick the lock..."
✅ "I attempt to pick the lock using my lockpick set..."

Let the GM determine success/failure.

### The Controller
❌ "I convince the mayor to tell me everything, then he helps me break into the mansion..."
✅ "I try to convince the mayor by appealing to his civic duty. I explain what I know and ask for his help."

Don't decide NPC actions - that's the GM's job.

### The Unprepared
❌ Submitting at the last minute with no time to edit
✅ Submit early, review later, edit as needed

## Advanced Tips

### Include Fallback Plans

Show the GM what you'll do if your first plan fails:

> "I try to pick the lock. If I can't get it open in 5 minutes, I'll give up and try the basement window instead. If that's also locked, I'll abort and return tomorrow with better tools."

### Set Boundaries

Tell the GM what your character WILL or WON'T do:

> "I'm willing to break into an abandoned building, but I won't hurt anyone, and I'll flee rather than fight if confronted."

### Ask Questions

If something is unclear, ask in your action:

> "Is there a guard at the main gate? If so, I'll use the side entrance. If not, I'll walk right up to the front door."

### Reference Previous Results

Build on what you learned:

> "Based on the journal entry I found last phase about the oak tree near Miller's Creek, I'll go there and search for the symbol carved into it."

### Use Resources

Mention equipment, skills, or advantages:

> "I'm using my journalism credentials to access the archives. My press badge has gotten me into restricted areas before."

## Working With Your GM

### What GMs Like

✅ **Specific actions** - Easy to adjudicate
✅ **Character voice** - Makes writing results fun
✅ **Reasonable goals** - Not trying to solve everything at once
✅ **Engagement** - Shows you care about the game
✅ **Timely submission** - Respects everyone's time

### What GMs Don't Like

❌ **Last-minute submissions** - Stressful for GM
❌ **Vague actions** - Hard to write good results
❌ **Controlling NPCs** - That's the GM's job
❌ **Ignoring previous results** - Breaks continuity
❌ **Complaining about results** - GM made a ruling, accept it

### If You Disagree With Results

Sometimes results aren't what you expected:

**Good response:**
> "Hey GM, I'm confused about the result. I thought my character would have noticed the guard before approaching. Can you clarify what happened?"

**Bad response:**
> "That's BS! My character is a trained detective, there's no way they'd miss that!"

**Remember:**
- The GM has information you don't
- Results reflect the fictional reality, not your preferences
- Respectful questions are fine, demands aren't
- Trust that the GM is trying to make a good story

## Tips for Success

### Submit Early

Submit a draft action early:
- You can always edit it
- Reduces last-minute stress
- Shows GM you're engaged
- Gives you time to revise

### Read the Common Room

Before writing your action:
- Review recent Common Room posts
- Check if plans changed
- See what other characters know
- Make sure you're not contradicting public statements

### Consider Consequences

Think about:
- What could go wrong?
- Who might notice what you're doing?
- What you'll do if you're caught
- How this affects future phases

### Stay In Character

Your action should sound like your character:
- Use their skills and knowledge
- Reflect their personality
- Follow their goals and motivations
- Make choices they would make

### Communicate

If something comes up:
- Tell your GM ASAP
- Don't ghost during an action phase
- Request extensions early
- Be honest about constraints

## Quick Reference

### Action Phase Checklist

Before submitting:
- [ ] Read GM's phase description
- [ ] Review Common Room for plans
- [ ] Write specific action with clear goal
- [ ] Include contingencies for failure
- [ ] Mention relevant skills/resources
- [ ] State what you hope to learn/accomplish
- [ ] Check spelling and clarity
- [ ] Submit early (can edit later)

### Typical Timeline

- **Day 1**: Phase starts, players begin planning
- **Days 2-5**: Common Room discussion, action writing
- **Day 6**: Deadline approaches, final edits
- **Day 7**: Deadline, GM starts writing results
- **Days 8-10**: Results delivered
- **Next phase**: Common Room to discuss results
