# Private Messages

Private Messages let you communicate secretly with other characters - planning conspiracies, sharing secrets, building relationships, or coordinating actions without anyone else knowing. They're a key tool for creating drama, intrigue, and authentic character interactions.

## What Are Private Messages?

Private Messages are **character-to-character communications** that:
- Are completely private to other players
- The GM and audience can see them
- Can be sent to one or multiple characters
- Persist throughout the game
- Are available only during common room
- Are publicly visible after the game ends

## Why Use Private Messages?

### Secret Planning
Coordinate actions without tipping off other players:

> **To: Marcus**
>
> Don't mention this in the publicly, but I found something weird in the library records. The mansion's ownership changed hands three times in the last year, always for exactly $1. That's not normal.
>
> I'm going to investigate the property office tomorrow. Want to come with?

### Character Relationships
Build one-on-one relationships and dynamics:

> **To: Elena**
>
> I noticed you were quiet during the planning session. Are you having second thoughts about breaking into the mansion? I don't blame you - this whole thing is getting dangerous.
>
> If you want to back out, I'll cover for you. Just say the word.

### Information Control
Share information selectively:

> **To: Chen**
>
> Last night I saw the mayor leaving the mansion at 3 AM. I'm not sharing this publicly yet - if the mayor is involved, we need to be careful about who knows what.
>
> What did your investigation turn up?

### Betrayal and Intrigue
Create dramatic moments (use responsibly!):

> **To: Kira**
>
> I know we agreed to split whatever we find 50/50, but I'm keeping the artifact. You can have the cash. Non-negotiable.
>
> Don't make a scene about it.

## How to Send a Private Message

### Basic Steps

1. Navigate to your game page
2. Click **Messages** tab
3. Click **New Message**
4. Select recipient(s)
5. Write your message
6. Click **Send**

### Message Components

**Recipients (Required)**:
- Select one or more characters
- Only active characters in the game can receive messages
- NPCs are valid choices, whether they are GM or Audience controlled

**Title (Required)**:
- Brief summary of the message topic
- Helps organize message threads
- Example: "Tonight's plan", "What I found", "We need to talk"

## Reading and Replying

### When You Receive a Message

You'll typically get:
- In-game notification badge
- Alert on the Messages page

**To read:**
1. Go to Messages tab
2. Click on the message thread
3. Read the conversation

### Replying to Messages

Messages are organized in **chat-style conversations**:

1. Open the message thread
2. Type your reply at the bottom
3. Click **Send**

Your reply stays in the same thread, creating a conversation history.

## Group Messages

You can send messages to multiple characters:

**To: Elena, Chen**

> Just wanted to coordinate - if we're all investigating different parts of the mystery, we should share what we find. But let's keep this between us three. Marcus seems... unreliable.

All recipients can see all replies. It's like a group chat.

## When to Use Private Messages vs. Common Room

### Use Private Messages For:

**Secret Plans:**
- ✅ "Let's search the mansion while Marcus distracts the mayor"
- ✅ "I'm going to betray the group and take the artifact for myself"
- ✅ "Meet me at the docks at midnight. Don't tell anyone."

**Sensitive Information:**
- ✅ "I saw you leaving the crime scene"
- ✅ "I found evidence that incriminates your character"
- ✅ "I know your character's secret backstory"

**Personal Relationships:**
- ✅ "I don't trust Marcus. Do you?"
- ✅ "My character has feelings for your character"
- ✅ "Can we talk about what happened last night?"

**Selective Sharing:**
- ✅ "Here's what I learned, but don't share this with Kira"
- ✅ "I'm going to lie in the Common Room about what I found"

### Use Common Room For:

**Group Coordination:**
- ✅ "Here's the plan for everyone"
- ✅ "Who wants to investigate the library?"
- ✅ "Meeting at the tavern at 8 PM"

**Public Information:**
- ✅ "I found a clue about the symbol"
- ✅ "The mayor is acting suspicious"
- ✅ "We should search the mansion"

**Character Development:**
- ✅ Public character moments and reactions
- ✅ Group debates and discussions
- ✅ Establishing your character's public personality

**Transparency:**
- ✅ When you want everyone to know
- ✅ When coordination requires full group buy-in

## Message Etiquette

### Be Respectful

**Do:**
- ✅ Write in character
- ✅ Keep player-vs-player conflict in-character
- ✅ Respect other players' boundaries
- ✅ Remember it's a game

**Don't:**
- ❌ Use private messages for out-of-character drama
- ❌ Harass other players
- ❌ Bring real-world conflicts into the game
- ❌ Use messages to bully or exclude

### Respond Reasonably

**Good Response Time:**
- Check messages during active phases
- Respond within a few hours when possible
- Communicate if you'll be away

**Don't Ghost:**
- If you receive a message, acknowledge it
- Even a "got it, thinking about this" is better than silence
- Not responding can stall other players' plans

### Stay in Character

**Strong Character Voice:**
> *Kira's message buzzes at 2 AM.*
>
> "Change of plans. The mayor knows we're onto him. Meet me at the old warehouse instead - it's safer. And Marcus? Leave him out of this. He's a liability."

**Weak (Sounds Like a Player):**
> "Hey, I think we should change the plan because the GM probably expects us to go to the mansion. Let's meta-game this differently."

### Don't Metagame

**Metagaming** = Using out-of-character knowledge your character wouldn't have.

**Bad Example:**
> **To: Marcus**
>
> "I know from your action submission that you're planning to betray us, so I'm going to stop you."

Your character can't know what's in Marcus's private action submission!

**Good Example:**
> **To: Marcus**
>
> "You've been acting weird lately. Are you hiding something from us?"

Based on Marcus's behavior in the Common Room, which your character CAN observe.

## Strategic Use of Private Messages

### Creating Information Asymmetry

Different characters knowing different things creates drama:

**Common Room (Public):**
> **Marcus**: "I searched the library but didn't find anything useful."

**Private Message to Kira:**
> **Marcus**: "I lied in the Common Room. I found proof the mayor is involved. But I don't trust Elena and Chen yet - let's keep this between us."

**Result**: Information splits create tension and interesting choices.

### Coordinating Sub-Groups

Form alliances within the larger group:

**To: Elena, Chen**

> I think we should form our own investigation team. Marcus and Kira are too reckless - they're going to get us all caught or killed.
>
> Let's coordinate our actions separately and share information only among us three. We'll feed the others just enough to keep them useful.

### Building Character Dynamics

Use messages to develop one-on-one relationships:

**Example: Mentor/Student Dynamic**

> **To: Chen**
>
> I've been doing this kind of work for twenty years, kid. Here's my advice: don't trust anyone completely, always have an exit strategy, and never let them see you're afraid.
>
> You're green, but you're smart. Stick with me and you might survive this.

**Example: Rivalry**

> **To: Kira**
>
> I saw the way you looked at the artifact. We both want it. But only one of us is going to walk away with it.
>
> May the best thief win.

### Planning Betrayals

If your character plans to betray the group, private messages set it up:

**Week 1 - To: Marcus**
> "I agree with your plan. Let's work together on this."

**Week 2 - To: Marcus**
> "I'm having second thoughts about Elena. She's asking too many questions."

**Week 3 - To: Marcus**
> "Change of plan. I'm taking the artifact myself. You can join me or get out of my way."

**Then in your Action Submission:**
> "I'm going to double-cross Marcus and take the artifact while he's distracted..."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### The Novel
❌ Writing 5000-word essays in private messages
✅ Keep messages focused and readable

### The Out-of-Character Chat
❌ "Hey dude, did you see the game last night? Also what's your character doing?"
✅ Stay in character - save OOC chat for Discord/other channels

### The Meta-Plotter
❌ "Let's coordinate our action submissions to game the system"
✅ Plan what your characters would actually coordinate

### The Ghoster
❌ Never responding to messages
✅ Reply within a reasonable timeframe

### The Over-Sharer
❌ Sending the same information to everyone individually
✅ Use Common Room for information meant for everyone

### The Paranoid Plotter
❌ Never trusting anyone, making every plan secret
✅ Balance secret plotting with group coordination - games need both

## Coordinating with Action Submissions

### The Dance of Secret Planning

**1. Plan in Private Messages:**
> **To: Kira** - "Let's both investigate the mansion tonight. I'll take the first floor, you take the basement."

**2. Submit Individual Actions:**
> **Your Action**: "I'm going to search the mansion's first floor tonight around 10 PM..."
> **Kira's Action**: "I'm heading to the mansion basement at 10 PM to search for evidence..."

**3. Get Results:**
> **GM to You**: "While searching the first floor, you hear footsteps in the basement - probably Kira. You also find a hidden safe..."

**4. Share Results in Private:**
> **To: Kira** - "I found a safe on the first floor. What did you find in the basement?"

**5. Decide What to Share Publicly:**
> **Common Room**: "Kira and I searched the mansion last night. We found some interesting things."

### When Plans Don't Survive Contact

**The Message:**
> **To: Marcus** - "I'll create a distraction at the front door at midnight so you can sneak in the back."

**Your Action:**
> "I approach the front door and knock loudly, making a scene..."

**The Result:**
> "When you knock, the door immediately opens. The mayor is standing there with two security guards. They grab you before you can run."

**The Follow-Up:**
> **To: Marcus** - "The plan went sideways. I got caught. Did you make it inside?"

Sometimes plans fail - that's part of the drama!

## Troubleshooting

### "No One Responds to My Messages"

**Possible Issues:**
- Messages are too vague or don't require responses
- You're messaging inactive players
- You're messaging people whose characters have no reason to trust you
- Timing - people are often spread across time zones

**Solutions:**
- Ask specific questions that require answers
- Choose active players to coordinate with
- Build relationships in Common Room first
- Give people time to respond

### "Someone is Harassing Me via Messages"

**What to Do:**
- Document the messages (screenshot)
- Contact the GM immediately (via Discord)
- If serious, contact platform moderators/admins
- Remember: Player conflict ≠ Character conflict

**What NOT to Do:**
- Don't engage in arguments
- Don't retaliate
- Don't quit without reporting it

### "I Accidentally Revealed Secret Plans"

**If you meant to send privately but posted in Common Room:**
- Acknowledge the mistake in-character if possible
- Adapt your plan - assume everyone knows now
- Learn from it - double-check recipient before sending

**Can't be undone**, but can be worked into the story!

### "I Want to Change My Message After Sending"

**Most platforms don't allow editing messages** for integrity reasons.

**If you need to correct:**
- Send a follow-up clarifying
- If it's a major mistake, contact the GM
- You can delete the message after it was sent

### When to Use Private Messages

- ✅ Secret plans and betrayals
- ✅ Sharing information selectively
- ✅ Character relationship development
- ✅ Coordinating with specific people

- Write in character
- Be specific when making plans
- Confirm plans before deadlines
- Balance secrets with public cooperation
- Respect player boundaries
