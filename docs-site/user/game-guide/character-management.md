# Character Management

Your character is your avatar in the game world - their personality, skills, backstory, and relationships bring the story to life. Managing your character effectively means keeping their information current, tracking their development, and knowing when to retire them or create new ones.

## Character Basics

### What is a Character?

A character is:
- **Your representation** in the game world
- **A persona** with their own goals, personality, and backstory
- **The lens** through which you experience the story
- **Your contribution** to the collaborative narrative

**Key Components:**
- Name and basic information
- Character concept and backstory
- Skills, abilities, or attributes (game-dependent)

## Character Status and Lifecycle

### Character States

Your character moves through different states during their lifecycle:

**Pending** 📝
- Character is being created
- Can be edited freely
- Not visible to other players
- Not part of the game yet

**Published** ✅
- Approved by GM and playing
- Participates in phases
- Can submit actions
- Visible to other players
- Can send/receive messages
-
### The Character Lifecycle

```
Create Draft → Submit for Approval → GM Reviews →
→ [Approved] → Active Play
```

## Creating Your Character

### Starting a New Character

1. Navigate to your game page
2. Click **People** and then **Characters**
3. Click **Create Character**
4. Fill out the character form
5. Submit for approval when ready

### What to Include

**Name** (Required):
- Character's name
- Can be a pseudonym or nickname
  - This is the publicly visible name, you can have their real name in your private notes
- Should fit the game setting

**Public Profile** (Required):
- Age
- Appearance
  - Include an avatar upload
- Any immediately obvious information about your character (gruff, energetic, awkward grin, etc.)

**Private Profile** (Required):
- Character's history and background
- What brought them to this story
- Key personality traits
- Goals and motivations

### Example Character Creation

**Name:** Sarah Chen

**Concept:** Investigative journalist who gets too close to dangerous stories

**Backstory:**
> Sarah Chen spent ten years covering local news before stumbling onto something big - a pattern of disappearances the police dismissed as runaways. When her editor killed the story, she quit and went independent.
>
> Now she survives on freelance work while chasing the truth about the disappearances. She's smart, persistent, and willing to break rules to get answers. But she's also reckless, paranoid, and struggling financially.
>
> Sarah's looking for the big story that will make her career - even if it gets her killed.

**Appearance:**
> Late 30s, Asian-American, usually dressed in practical clothes - jeans, boots, leather jacket. Always carrying a worn messenger bag with her laptop, camera, and notebook. Dark circles under her eyes.

## Submitting for Approval

### The Approval Process

**Why Approval is Needed:**
- Ensures character fits the game setting
- Prevents overpowered or problematic characters
- Confirms character works with the planned story
- Maintains game balance

**What GMs Look For:**
- Fits the game setting and tone
- Clear motivations and goals
- Hooks the GM can use in the story
- Won't disrupt other players' experience

### How to Submit

1. Review your character draft
2. Check it against GM's character guidelines
3. Message your GM
4. Wait for GM feedback


### Getting Feedback

**Possible GM Responses:**

✅ **Approved** - Your character is active and can play

📝 **Revisions Requested** - GM asks for changes
> "Love the concept! Can you tone down the combat skills a bit? This is more investigation-focused. Also, can you add more about why she's in this specific town?"

❌ **Rejected** - Character doesn't fit
> "This concept doesn't work for this game - we're doing 1920s noir, not cyberpunk. Want to try a different concept?"

### Making Revisions

If GM requests changes:

1. You can edit freely
2. Make the requested changes
3. Resubmit for approval

**Good Response to Feedback:**
> "Thanks for the feedback! I've toned down the combat skills and added backstory about why she's in Arkham investigating her missing sister. Resubmitting now."

**Bad Response:**
> "But my character needs those skills! This is unfair!"

Remember: The GM is trying to make the game work for everyone.

## Managing Active Characters

### Viewing Your Character

**Character Sheet:**
- Name and basic info
- Current status
- Backstory and skills
- Inventory, abilities, and currencies

**Where to Find:**
- People tab on game page
- Character profile link

### Updating Your Character

**What You Can Update:**
- Appearance/description (usually)
- Character notes (private)

**What You Usually Can't Update:**
- Core backstory (would break continuity)
- Skills/attributes (handled by the GM)
- Name (requires GM approval)

**How to Request Changes:**
- Message your GM
- Explain why the change is needed
- Wait for approval

**Example Request:**
> "GM, I'd like to add a 'Research' skill to Sarah's sheet based on all the library investigation she's been doing. Does that work?"

### Character Notes

Keeping notes is a good idea, but that feature is not included in the Action Phase platform. Many people use Google docs to create a shareable document for their notes.

### Updating Relationships

As your character develops relationships:

**Track in Character Notes:**
- Who your character trusts
- Who they're suspicious of
- Rivalries and conflicts

**Reflect in Roleplay:**
- Common Room posts show relationship changes
- Private messages deepen connections
- Actions reflect trust or suspicion

## Multiple Characters

### Can You Have Multiple Characters?

**Most games allow one of:**
- **One active character per game** (most common)
- **Multiple active characters** (rare, GM-dependent)

**Check your game's rules** before creating multiple characters.

## Character Relationships

### Tracking Relationships

**With Other PCs:**
- Trust levels
- Shared experiences
- Conflicts and alliances

**With NPCs:**
- Contacts and allies
- Enemies and rivals
- Neutral parties

### Developing Relationships

**Through Common Room:**
- Public debates and discussions
- Showing character personality
- Reacting to others' posts

**Through Private Messages:**
- One-on-one conversations
- Secret alliances
- Personal revelations

**Through Actions:**
- Helping or betraying others
- Coordinated plans
- Shared dangers

## Character Privacy and Visibility

### What Others Can See

**Other Players Can See:**
- ✅ Character name and description
- ✅ Common Room posts
- ✅ Public character information

**Other Players Cannot See:**
- ❌ Your action submissions
- ❌ Your private character notes
- ❌ Abilities, skills, or inventory
- ❌ GM results sent to you
- ❌ Your private messages

**GM and Audience Can See:**
- ✅ Everything

## Character Sheet Updates

### Updating Backstory

**Generally not allowed** because it would retcon established story.

**Exception:**
- Revealing previously hidden backstory elements
- GM-approved flashbacks or revelations

**Example:**
> "I'd like to reveal that Sarah has a secret - she's the mayor's daughter. Can I add this to her backstory as something she's kept hidden from everyone?"

## Common Character Mistakes

### The Mary Sue/Gary Stu
❌ Character is perfect, never fails, everyone loves them
✅ Character has flaws, makes mistakes, faces consequences

### The Lone Wolf
❌ "My character doesn't trust anyone and works alone always"
✅ Character has trust issues but gradually opens up

### The Spotlight Hog
❌ Character dominates every scene and conversation
✅ Character shares spotlight, supports others' moments

### The Passive Observer
❌ Character never takes initiative or makes decisions
✅ Character drives story forward with active choices

### The Metagamer
❌ Character knows things the player knows but character doesn't
✅ Character only acts on information they've actually learned

### The Unchanging
❌ Character is exactly the same at end as beginning
✅ Character grows, learns, changes through experience

## Tips for Great Characters

### Make Them Proactive

**Weak Character:**
> "I guess I'll go along with whatever the group decides."

**Strong Character:**
> "I think we should investigate the mansion tonight. Who's with me?"

### Give Them Goals

**Every character should want something:**
- Find the truth
- Protect someone
- Get revenge
- Escape their past
- Prove themselves
- Gain power/knowledge

**Goals drive actions** and create interesting choices.

### Give Them Flaws

**Perfect characters are boring:**
- Arrogance
- Cowardice
- Addiction
- Trust issues
- Recklessness
- Greed

**Flaws create conflict** and make victories meaningful.

### Make Them Reactive

**Respond to events:**
- Other characters' actions
- GM's story developments
- Consequences of their choices

**Show emotion and change:**
- Fear, anger, joy, sadness
- Opinion shifts
- Relationship changes

### Create Hooks for Others

**Give other players something to work with:**

**Weak:**
> "My character stands in the corner silently."

**Strong:**
> "Sarah paces nervously, muttering to herself while reviewing her notes. She looks like she's about to say something, then stops herself."

This invites others to ask, "What's wrong?" or "What did you find?"

## Character Management Checklist

### Before Creating New Character

- [ ] Read GM's character creation guidelines
- [ ] Check game setting/tone
- [ ] Draft complete backstory
- [ ] Identify character goals and flaws

## Troubleshooting

### "My Character Feels Boring"

**Try:**
- Give them a secret
- Create conflict with another character
- Make a risky choice
- Reveal hidden backstory
- Change their opinion on something important

### "My Character Doesn't Fit the Story"

**Options:**
- Talk to GM about adjusting the backstory
- Find new angle for existing character
- Embrace being the outsider

### "I Want to Change My Character Concept"

**If Minor:**
- Just start playing them differently
- Character growth is natural

**If Major:**
- Talk to GM
- May require narrative event that transforms them

### "Another Player's Character is Problematic"

**If in-character conflict:**
- Good! Conflict creates story
- Engage with it through roleplay

**If player behavior issue:**
- Talk to GM privately
- Document specific problems
- Request GM intervention

Don't try to solve player problems in-character.
