# Frequently Asked Questions

## Getting Started

### What is ActionPhase?

ActionPhase is a platform for playing phase-based, text-based roleplaying games online. Think of it as a blend between play-by-post forum RPGs and modern async gaming - you collaborate with other players to create a story, guided by a Game Master (GM).

Unlike real-time tabletop games, ActionPhase games progress in phases over days or weeks, making them easier for people with busy schedules.

**Note:** Common Room phases often have a lot of activity. You don't need to be active 24/7, but it will be up to you to decide how much time you want to spend in-character.

### Do I need any experience with RPGs to play?

No! While experience with roleplaying games helps, ActionPhase is designed to be accessible to newcomers. Our guides walk you through everything you need to know, and most GMs are happy to help new players learn.

### How much time does it take to play?

This depends heavily on how much you want to participate in the game's common room phases.

At a minimum, you should expect to leave at least 1-2 comments per Common Room phase as well as submit your Action Phase action.

Beyond that, the sky is the limit. Want to spend huge chunks of time in-character? Go for it! Prefer to keep to yourself and have a few private conversations? No problem.

The beauty of ActionPhase is that you can participate on your own schedule - no need to coordinate real-time sessions.

### Is ActionPhase free?

Yes! ActionPhase is free to use for both players and Game Masters. Currently hosting costs are minimal, but if that changes I may open up donations to help keep things afloat.

## Finding & Joining Games

### How do I find games to join?

1. Browse the game list on the home page
2. Filter by status (recruiting)
3. Read game descriptions to find one that interests you
4. Click to view details and apply

See: [Joining a Game guide](/user/getting-started/joining-game)

### What does "Recruiting" mean?

A game in "Recruiting" phase is actively accepting new player applications. The GM is looking for players and reviewing character submissions.

### How do I apply to join a game?

Click the "Apply" or "Join Game" button on the game page.

### Can I join multiple games?

Yes! You can participate in as many games as you can reasonably keep up with. Just be realistic about your time commitment.

### Can I leave a game?

Yes, but this is generally frowned upon. You can leave a game at any time from the game page. Please let your GM know - it's courteous and helps them plan.

## Characters

### How do I create a character?

See our [First Character guide](/user/getting-started/first-character) for detailed instructions.

Quick version:
1. Join a game
2. Click "Create Character"
3. Fill out the character form (name, backstory)
4. Notify GM for review
5. Wait for GM to review

### Can I have multiple characters in the same game?

Usually no - most games allow one active character per player. Some GMs may allow multiple characters in special circumstances.

### Can I edit my character after approval?

Minor edits (fixing typos, updating description) are usually fine. Major changes (backstory, skills, personality) require GM approval. Ask your GM first.

### What happens if my character dies?

Character death is permanent. Depending on the game:
- You might create a new character to continue playing
- You might spectate the rest of the game
- The game might end soon anyway

Talk to your GM about what happens next.

## Playing the Game

### What are phases?

Phases are the structure of the game. Games alternate between two phase types:
- **Common Room Phase:** Public discussion, planning, character interaction
- **Action Phase:** Private submission of what your character does, followed by GM publishing results

After the GM publishes results for an Action Phase, they create a new Common Room Phase and the cycle continues.

See: [Game Phases guide](/user/game-guide/game-phases)

### How long do phases last?

It varies by game! Typical lengths:
- Common Room: 1-3 days
- Action Phase: 3-5 days (includes time for GM to write and publish results)

After the Action Phase deadline, the GM typically takes 3-5 days to write and publish results, then creates the next Common Room phase.

Check your game's description or ask your GM.

### What if I miss a deadline?

If you miss an Action Phase deadline:
- Your character takes no action that phase
- The story continues without you
- You can still participate in the next phase

Communicate with your GM if you need an extension!

### Can other players see my actions?

**No!** Action submissions are private between you and the GM. Other players only see:
- What you post in the Common Room
- Your character's public information
- Results that affect them

This privacy is intentional - it creates suspense and allows for secrets.

Audience members can see your character's private messages and private actions at any time.

### Can the GM see my actions before the deadline?

Yes, but your submission can be edited until the deadline and won't be locked in until then.

### How do I coordinate with other players?

**In Common Room:**
- Discuss plans openly
- Agree on who does what

**In Private Messages:**
- Make secret alliances
- Share private information
- Coordinate without others knowing

**In Actions:**
- Reference the agreed plan
- Or deviate from it if your character would!

See: [Common Room guide](/user/game-guide/common-room)

### What if I want to do something secret?

Depends on the timing. If you are in a Common Room phase, you can start a Private Conversation with the Gamemaster character.

If you're in an Action Phase, submit it in your action! Other players won't see it. You can:
- Betray the group
- Investigate someone secretly
- Make alliances others don't know about
- Hide information

The GM will handle it in results.

### Can I change my action after submitting?

**Before the deadline:** Yes! Edit as many times as you want.

**After the deadline:** No. Actions lock at the deadline.

Submit early and revise as needed!

## Common Room

### What is the Common Room?

The Common Room is the public discussion space where all players interact in-character. Think of it as your game's forum or chat room.

See: [Common Room guide](/user/game-guide/common-room)

### Do I have to post in character?

Yes! The Common Room is for in-character roleplay. Out-of-character chat should happen in the game's OOC area (for example, Discord channel if your game has one).

### How often should I post?

Most GMs expect 2-3 Common Room posts per phase. Check your game's expectations. Don't feel pressured to post constantly, but do engage enough to stay connected to the group. If you want to post more, you certainly can!

### What if I'm not sure what to say?

**Easy posts:**
- Respond to what others said
- Ask another character a question
- Describe what your character is doing
- React to recent events

You don't need to write a masterpiece - just engage!

### Can I edit my posts?

Yes, you can edit Common Room posts to fix typos or clarify. However:
- Don't change what you said after others responded
- Don't use editing to retcon major decisions
- Be respectful of the conversation flow

## Private Messages

### What are Private Messages?

A way to communicate with other players (in-character) without everyone seeing. Perfect for secret alliances, private conversations, or sharing information selectively.

See: [Private Messages guide](/user/game-guide/private-messages)

### Can the GM see my private messages?

**Yes.** The GM and audience members can see all private messages.

Once the game has ended, all players will be able to see all private messages.

### Can I lie in private messages?

Yes! Your character can lie, mislead, or hide information from other characters. That's part of the game.

### Should I share my action with other players?

Only if you want to! You can:
- Keep actions completely secret
- Share with trusted allies
- Coordinate detailed plans
- Mislead others about your plans

## Game Master Questions

### What does a GM do?

The GM (Game Master) creates the game world, writes story prompts, responds to player actions with personalized results, controls NPCs, and keeps the game moving.

See: [GM Getting Started guide](/user/gm-guide/getting-started)

### Can I become a GM?

Yes! Anyone can create and run games. Start with our [GM guides](/user/gm-guide/getting-started) to learn how.

### Do I need experience to GM?

Helpful, but not required! Many first-time GMs run great games. Start small and learn as you go.

### How much work is GMing?

Depends on the game type. Some GMs are experienced, others are new to the game.

In general, expect that writing result PMs will take a LOT of time.

During Common Room phases, you'll be handling character actions and playing NPCs.

There is no wrong way to run a game - it's up to you! Don't want result PMs? No problem! Prefer to be absent during common room? Mostly fine, but you should still be prepared to step in.

### Can I pause or end my game?

Yes! Life happens. You can:
- **Pause:** Announce a break, resume later
- **End early:** Wrap up with a final phase
- **Hand off:** Ask another player to take over (rare)

Communicate with your players - they'll usually understand.

## Technical Questions

### What devices can I use?

ActionPhase works on:
- Desktop browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
- Tablets
- Smartphones

The interface is responsive and works on all screen sizes.

### Do I need to download anything?

No! ActionPhase is entirely web-based. Just visit the site and play.

### Can I use ActionPhase offline?

No, ActionPhase requires an internet connection to sync with other players and the game.

### Is there a mobile app?

No, but the website works great on mobile browsers!

### What if I find a bug?

Report it! Reach out on Discord with:
- What you were doing
- What happened (vs. what you expected)
- Screenshots if possible

## Troubleshooting

### I can't submit my action

Common causes:
- Deadline has passed (actions lock)
- You're not in an Action Phase
- Browser issue (try refreshing)
- Network connectivity problem

### My character isn't showing up

- Did the GM approve it? Check character status
- Are you viewing the right game?
- Try refreshing the page

### The page won't load

Try:
1. Refresh the page
2. Clear your browser cache
3. Try a different browser
4. Check your internet connection

Still stuck? Reach out on Discord with details.

### I can't log in

**Password issues:**
- Use "Forgot Password" to reset
- Check that Caps Lock is off
- Try copying/pasting your password

**Account issues:**
- Verify your email address
- Check that you're using the correct username (case sensitive!)

## Advanced Topics

### What is metagaming and why is it bad?

**Metagaming** is when a player uses out-of-character knowledge that their character shouldn't have.

**Examples:**
- Reading other players' results and acting on that information
- Your character "coincidentally" shows up where the action is
- Knowing the monster's weakness because you read the rulebook

**Why avoid it:** It breaks immersion and ruins surprises. Keep player knowledge and character knowledge separate.

### How do I create dramatic conflict without being a jerk?

**In-character conflict is great:**
- Characters disagree on plans
- Different motivations create tension
- Personality clashes make interesting drama

**Keep it in-character:**
- You're not attacking the player, just playing your character
- Be willing to lose arguments
- Let others have moments too
- Talk to players OOC if concerned
