# Managing Players

As a GM, you have complete control over who participates in your game and in what capacity. This guide covers player applications, roles, and participant management.

## Participant Roles

ActionPhase supports three participant roles:

### Player
- Creates and controls one character
- Submits actions during Action Phases
- Posts in Common Room
- Cannot see other players' action submissions
- **Count toward max_players limit**

### Co-GM
- Assists the GM with game management
- Can approve characters
- Can create and activate phases
- Can post as NPCs
- Sees all action submissions
- **Does NOT count toward player limit**
- Great for large games or shared storytelling

### Audience
- Observes the game without participating
- Can see all phases and posts
- Cannot create characters or submit actions
- Cannot post in Common Room (read-only)
  - Can be assigned NPCs which allow Common Room and Private Message access
- **Does NOT count toward player limit**
- Useful for spectators or future players learning the game

## Managing Applications

### Reviewing Applications

When your game is in "Recruitment" state, players can apply:

**Application includes:**
- Username and account creation date
- Application text (optional - you can require this)

**To review applications:**
1. Go to your game page
2. Click the "Applications" tab or notification
3. Read each application
4. Click "Approve" or "Decline"
5. Application status is not published until you transition the game to "Character Creation"

### Application Auto-Accept (Audience)

If you enabled "Auto-Accept Audience Applications":
- Audience applications are automatically approved
- Player applications still require manual approval
- You can disable this setting anytime

## Adding Players Directly

You can bypass the application process by adding players directly:

### How to Add a Player

1. Go to your game's "People" tab
2. Click "Add Player" button (GM only)
3. Search for the user by username
4. Select them from the dropdown
5. Click "Add"

**What happens:**
- They're immediately added as a participant
- They skip the application process
- They can create a character right away
- Useful for friends or trusted players

**When to use this:**
- Private games with known players
- Inviting specific people
- Filling slots quickly
- Replacing a player who left

## Managing Existing Participants

### Viewing Participants

The "People" tab shows all participants:
- **Characters sub-tab:** All approved characters
- **Participants sub-tab:** All users and their roles

### Changing Roles

You can promote or demote participants:

**Promoting to Co-GM:**
1. Find the Audience Member in the Participants list
2. Click the menu icon (⋮)
3. Select "Promote to Co-GM"
4. Confirm the action

**Effects:**
- They gain GM privileges immediately
- They can see all player actions
- They can manage phases and characters

**Demoting a Co-GM:**
1. Find the Co-GM in the Participants list
2. Click the menu icon (⋮)
3. Select "Demote to Player"

**Effects:**
- They lose GM privileges
- They retain Audience access

### Removing Participants

**When to remove someone:**
- They asked to leave
- They're inactive and non-responsive
- They violated game rules or community guidelines
- The group agreed they're not a good fit

**How to remove:**
1. Find them in the Participants list
2. Click the menu icon (⋮)
3. Select "Remove from Game"
4. Confirm

**What happens:**
- They lose all access to the game
- Their character becomes inactive (not deleted)
- Their posts and actions remain visible (for continuity)
- They receive a notification

**Their data is preserved:**
- Character sheets remain (inactive)
- Common Room posts stay
- Action submissions stay
- Private messages are preserved
- Nothing is deleted
- You can re-assign the Character

### Handling Player Character Removal

When a player is removed or leaves:

**Their character becomes "inactive":**
- Appears in "Inactive Characters" section
- Can be viewed but not edited
- Maintains game continuity and history

**Options for the character:**

**1. Reassign to audience/NPC**
- Character becomes GM-controlled NPC
- Useful if character is central to plot

**2. Write them out**
- Common Room post explaining their departure
- "Character Name has left the investigation to care for family"
- Clean narrative closure

**3. Leave ambiguous**
- Just mark inactive
- No explanation needed
- They fade into background

## Participant Management Best Practices

### Setting Expectations Early

**In your game description, specify:**
- Expected posting frequency
- Time zone considerations
- Activity requirements
- Consequences for inactivity

**Example:**
> **Activity Expectations:** Post at least twice per Common Room phase (usually 2-3 days each). Submit actions before deadline. If you'll be unavailable for 7+ days, please notify the GM.

### Handling Inactive Players

**Before removing someone:**
1. Send them a private message
2. Give them 3-5 days to respond
3. Offer to work with their schedule
4. Only remove if they don't respond or can't commit

**Communication template:**
> Hey [Player], I've noticed you haven't posted in the last [timeframe]. Is everything okay? If you need to take a break or leave the game, no problem - just let me know. If you want to stay, I'd appreciate more active participation. Let me know what works for you!

### Managing Audience Members

**Audience members can:**
- Read everything (phases, common room, results)
- See all characters and sheets
- Cannot post or interact

**Why allow audience?**
- Transparency builds trust
- Potential future players can learn
- Friends want to follow along
- Creates community around your game
- Some people may not have the time to play, but still want to be involved

**Why restrict audience?**
- Private/intimate games
- Sensitive content or topics
- Small, tight-knit group preference
- Avoiding outside pressure or commentary

**Managing audience applications:**
- Enable auto-accept for open games
- Manual approval for selective observation
- Can remove audience members anytime (same as players)

### Co-GM Guidelines

**Good candidates for Co-GM:**
- Experienced ActionPhase users
- People who understand your vision
- Reliable and communicative
- Share workload for large games

**Dividing responsibilities:**
- One GM handles main story, one handles subplots
- One writes results, one manages phases/timing
- One handles character approval, one handles player questions
- Both review everything before publishing

**Communication is key:**
- Discuss major plot developments
- Coordinate on result writing
- Align on character approval standards
- Present unified front to players

## Common Scenarios

### "Someone stopped posting mid-game"

1. Message them privately (don't call them out publicly)
2. Wait 3-5 days for response
3. If urgent (blocking the game), allow others to continue
4. If non-responsive after a week, remove them
5. Handle their character narratively (write them out or make inactive)

### "Two players are in conflict"

1. Don't take sides publicly
2. Message each privately to understand the situation
3. Remind everyone of respectful behavior expectations
4. If it's a character conflict (in-game), that's fine!
5. If it's player conflict (out-of-game), mediate or remove if necessary

### "Player wants to leave"

1. Thank them for letting you know
2. Remove them promptly (don't make them ask twice)
3. Handle their character's departure narratively
4. No hard feelings - life happens
5. Leave door open if they want to return or join future games

### "I need to remove someone for bad behavior"

1. Remove them immediately if serious (harassment, abuse)
2. Document the reason for your records
3. Don't discuss publicly - handle privately
4. Report to admins if it violates platform rules

### "Game is full but someone great applied"

**Options:**
1. Accept them as audience (if they're willing)
2. Offer Co-GM role (if they'd be good at it)
3. Keep their application and contact them if someone leaves
4. Politely decline and invite them to future games

### "Can I add more players mid-game?"

Yes, but it's challenging:
- Requires narrative reason for new character
- New player needs to catch up on story
- Group dynamics may be already established
- Best during a Common Room phase for introductions

**How to do it well:**
1. Get existing players' buy-in first
2. Add during Common Room (not Action phase)
3. Provide new player with comprehensive summary
4. Have a narrative hook for their character's entrance
5. Give them extra attention in first few phases
